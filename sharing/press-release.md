**[CITY], March 26, 2026** — A comprehensive open-source simulation of the 2026 Iran War, built using AI research agents and modeling 18 resources, 18 countries, and 20 cascade pathways, predicts a global pharmaceutical supply crisis beginning in late April 2026 as buffer stocks for antibiotics and diabetes medications exhaust approximately 60 days after Hormuz Strait closure.

The simulation, available at [coderexpert123.github.io/war-games-2026](https://coderexpert123.github.io/war-games-2026/), models the war from physical resource constraints upward rather than from political assumptions downward. Its key findings challenge conventional analysis of the conflict's trajectory and outcomes.

**Key Findings:**

**Pharmaceutical Crisis Timeline:** The model predicts pharmaceutical buffer stocks will exhaust in late April 2026, approximately 60 days after Hormuz Strait closure. China controls 70% of India's active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) imports, and the WHO's Dubai logistics hub is currently paralyzed. Antibiotics and metformin are identified as the most exposed medications.

**Food Crisis Already Locked In:** Northern Hemisphere spring planting (March-May 2026) occurred with disrupted fertilizer supply, with one-third of global fertilizer trade transiting the affected strait. The model indicates that even an immediate ceasefire cannot reverse Q3-Q4 crop yield impacts, with 1 billion+ people facing food insecurity by late 2026.

**Insurance Multiplier Effect:** Lloyd's of London war-risk designations create a $1:$390 trade multiplier, meaning every dollar of insurance withdrawn freezes $390 in trade activity. Insurance designations are modeled to persist weeks to months after any ceasefire, extending economic impact beyond military operations.

**November 2026 Convergence:** Three independent pressure points converge between November 3-27, 2026: US midterm elections (with 53% public opposition to the war), China's gallium/germanium export ban suspension expiration (November 27), and EU winter gas storage deadline (December 1, with storage tracking at 55-70% versus 90% target).

**Strategic Winner Identification:** The simulation identifies China as the strategic winner across four of five modeled scenarios, based on control of rare earth processing (99% of gallium, 83% of germanium, 78% of cobalt refining), insulation from Hormuz disruption, and mediator positioning for post-war reconstruction.

**Cascade Compounding:** The model identifies semiconductor production facing three independent input crises simultaneously (helium 33% offline, bromine 67% at risk, neon combinatorial), producing a predicted 30-50% chip output cut with no single-source fix.

**AI Infrastructure Delay:** The $650 billion global AI infrastructure buildout is modeled to experience 6-12 month delays from combined helium/chip/energy disruptions.

The simulation was built using Claude Code (Anthropic) as an orchestration layer, deploying parallel research agents to investigate each resource, country, and cascade pathway independently. Each agent produced sourced analysis from major news outlets, think tanks, and government reports dated February-March 2026.
